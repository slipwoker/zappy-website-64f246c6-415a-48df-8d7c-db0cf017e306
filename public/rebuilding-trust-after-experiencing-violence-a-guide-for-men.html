The Experience of Transgender Men: An Additional Layer of Pain

For transgender men who have experienced violence because of their identity, the situation is even more complex. Often, those who were supposed to be sources of support – family, partners, therapeutic or religious frameworks – are the very ones who cause harm. Instead of acceptance, they encounter rejection, humiliation, and sometimes physical or verbal abuse.

In such cases, the question "Who will even believe me?" becomes central. There is fear that disclosure will lead to further rejection, ridicule, or denial of their gender identity. The search for a safe space, where one can be a man, a transgender individual, and a survivor of violence, becomes an exhausting task, sometimes seeming without solution.
